Exploration of 1930s Depression-Era Fashion

Fashion in the 1930s reflected a blend of practicality and elegance, shaped by the economic hardships of the Great Depression and the influence of Hollywood glamour. Clothing for both men and women became more streamlined and tailored, emphasizing clean lines and simplicity. Wool, cotton, and other affordable fabrics were common, while muted colors like browns, grays, and navy dominated everyday wear.Accessories, including hats, gloves, and belts, were used to add a touch of sophistication without excess.
